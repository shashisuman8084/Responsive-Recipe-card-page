Litti-chokha

About Litti -chokha

Litti Chokha is a traditional dish from Bihar, India, comprising roasted or baked wheat flour balls (litti) stuffed with a spiced gram flour (sattu) filling, served with a flavorful mashed vegetable side dish (chokha) made from roasted eggplant, tomatoes, and boiled potatoes.

The litti is drizzled with ghee for richness, while the chokha is enhanced with mustard oil and spices, creating a smoky and earthy flavor profile. Known for its simplicity and rustic charm, Litti Chokha is a nutritious and wholesome meal that represents the culinary heritage of Bihar.

Drizzled with ghee for added richness, this smoky, earthy, and wholesome dish embodies the rustic and flavorful culinary heritage of Bihar, India.

Litti is a baked or roasted whole wheat dough ball filled with a spiced mixture of roasted gram flour (sattu), spices, mustard oil, and sometimes pickled spices.

Ingredients for Litti Chokha:

    For Litti (Stuffed Wheat Balls):

  • Wheat Flour: 2 cups
  • Salt: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Ghee or Oil: 2 tablespoons (for dough and brushing)
  • Water: As needed (to knead the dough)

    • For the Stuffing (Sattu Mixture):

  • Sattu (Roasted Gram Flour): 1 cup
  • Ginger: 1 teaspoon, grated
  • Green Chilies: 2, finely chopped
  • Onion: 1 small, finely chopped
  • Ajwain (Carom Seeds): 1/2 teaspoon
  • Nigella Seeds (Kalonji): 1/4 teaspoon (optional)
  • Salt: To taste
  • Lemon Juice: 1 tablespoon
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves: 2 tablespoons, chopped

    • For Chokha (Mashed Vegetables):

    Main Vegetables:

  • Eggplant (Baingan): 1 large, roasted
  • Tomatoes: 2 large, roasted
  • Potatoes: 2 medium, boiled

    • Seasoning and Flavoring:

  • Mustard Oil: 2 tablespoon
  • Garlic: 3–4 cloves, minced
  • Green Chilies: 1–2, finely chopped
  • Green Chilies: 1–2, finely chopped
  • Salt: To taste
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves: 2 tablespoons, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente.
  2. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water, then drain the pasta and set aside.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  4. Add garlic (and onion if using). Sauté until fragrant and the onion is translucent (about 2–3 minutes).
  5. Stir in the tomatoes and let them cook for 5–7 minutes, breaking them down with a spoon until they form a sauce. (If using canned tomatoes, cook until the sauce thickens slightly.)
  6. Stir in basil, a pinch of salt, black pepper, and red chili flakes (if desired).
  7. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss to coat in the sauce.
  8. If adding vegetables (like mushrooms, bell peppers, or spinach), toss them into the skillet and cook until tender.
  9. If the sauce is too thick, add a little reserved pasta water to loosen it to your desired consistency.
  10. Sprinkle Parmesan cheese over the pasta and mix well until melted and combined.
  11. Transfer to serving plates, garnish with fresh basil leaves or parsley, and add extra Parmesan if desired.
