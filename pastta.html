Pastta Recipe

About Pasta recipe

Masala pasta is an indian-italian fusion dish that mayhave originated on the streets of india, selling it as a street food. Not a traditional dish but it has been a afavorite & made in almost everyindian home, especially with kids.

Surprisingly you see a lot of Moms customizing it their one wayto suit the kids'taste food

That being said,Masala pasta can be eaten by everyone-kids & growns-ups! This indian style flavorful & delicious masala pasta will be your new favorite! Because you can make it however you -like- spicy,less-spicy but flavorsome, creamy or meaty.

This dish makes for a comforting weeknight dinner & can also be taken in the school box for lunch.It can also be cooked and refrigetrated for 2 days. Reheat it with a splash of water on the stovetop or in the instant pot.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups pasta
  • 2 tablespoon olive oil or butter(divide)(adjust to taste)
  • Garlic: 2–3 cloves, minced
  • Tomatoes: 2 large fresh tomatoes (chopped) or 1 cup of canned diced tomatoes
  • Basil: Fresh leaves (a handful) or 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Parmesan cheese: 1/4 cup, grated
  • Salt: To taste
  • Black pepper: To taste
  • Red chili flakes: 1/2 teaspoon (optional, for a bit of heat)
  • Onion: 1 small, finely chopped (optional)
  • Vegetables (optional): Mushrooms, bell peppers, or spinach

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente.
  2. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water, then drain the pasta and set aside.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  4. Add garlic (and onion if using). Sauté until fragrant and the onion is translucent (about 2–3 minutes).
  5. Stir in the tomatoes and let them cook for 5–7 minutes, breaking them down with a spoon until they form a sauce. (If using canned tomatoes, cook until the sauce thickens slightly.)
  6. Stir in basil, a pinch of salt, black pepper, and red chili flakes (if desired).
  7. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss to coat in the sauce.
  8. If adding vegetables (like mushrooms, bell peppers, or spinach), toss them into the skillet and cook until tender.
  9. If the sauce is too thick, add a little reserved pasta water to loosen it to your desired consistency.
  10. Sprinkle Parmesan cheese over the pasta and mix well until melted and combined.
  11. Transfer to serving plates, garnish with fresh basil leaves or parsley, and add extra Parmesan if desired.
