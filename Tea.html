Tea

About tea

Tea is a popular beverage made by steeping the processed leaves, buds, or twigs of the Camellia sinensis plant in hot water

Renowned for its rich history and cultural significance worldwide, tea comes in various types, including black, green, white, oolong, and herbal infusions, each offering unique flavors and health benefits.

t is enjoyed plain or with additions like milk, sugar, lemon, or spices, making it a versatile and comforting drink that can be tailored to personal preferences.

Ingredients for Basic Tea:

  • Water: 1 cup (per serving)
  • Tea Leaves or Tea Bags: 1 teaspoon of loose tea leaves or 1 tea bag (per serving)
  • Sugar: As per taste (optional)
  • Milk: 1/4 to 1/2 cup (optional, for milk tea)

    • Optional Ingredients (for Flavor Variations):

  • Ginger: 1 small piece, crushed (for ginger tea)
  • Cardamom: 1–2 pods, crushed (for spiced tea)
  • Cinnamon: 1 small stick (for a warm flavor)
  • Cloves: 1–2 (optional)
  • Lemon: 1 slice or a few drops of lemon juice (for lemon tea)
  • Honey: As a sweetener alternative to sugar

Instructions:

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of tea leaves or 1 tea bag.
  3. Simmer for 2–3 minutes (add sugar, ginger, or spices if desired).
  4. Add 1/4–1/2 cup milk (optional) and boil for another minute.
  5. Strain into a cup and serve hot.
